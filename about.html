Lip Blush Watford

Lip blush is a semi-permanent cosmetic tattooing treatment designed to enhance the natural color, shape, and definition of the lips. This procedure creates a soft, tinted look—similar to a lip tint or balm—that gives the lips a more youthful and symmetrical appearance.

Before the procedure begins, a numbing cream is applied to the lips to minimize discomfort. Once the area is partially numb, pigment is carefully deposited into the top layer of the skin using a fine needle. The process is precise and customizable, ensuring a natural finish that complements your features.

Prior to your treatment, you will complete a consultation form to assess your suitability based on any contraindications. If no contraindications are present, a patch test will be provided to ensure safety. After successful test results, we’ll proceed with the treatment. During the session, we will analyze your skin type, preferred shapes, and tone using the Fitzpatrick scale to determine the perfect pigment. Located in Watford, Hertfordshire, our studio provides a relaxing environment where sessions last approximately 2-3 hours, depending on the treatment area. Each procedure includes precise measurements and pre-drawing for symmetry and accuracy. A numbing agent is applied to ensure comfort, and the results are tailored to enhance your natural features. Post-treatment, a healing period of 4-6 weeks allows the pigment to settle. During this time, any necessary touch-ups can refine the results. With proper care, semi-permanent makeup offers a smudge-free, time-saving solution lasting 1-3 years, empowering you with a polished, confident look

Contraindications

PLEASE BE AWARE: The procedure cannot take place if any of the below are present.

  • Pregnant/Breastfeeding: No exceptions.
  • Acne: Hormones affect healing color of brows. Lip blush is allowed.
  • Hemophilia: Bleeding disorder.
  • Keloid Scarring History: Requires doctor’s note for clearance.
  • Cold Sores: OK if antiviral medication is taken prior to appointment.
  • Recent Lip Fillers: Must wait at least 6 weeks.
  • Accutane/Steroids: Must wait 1 year after finishing medication.
  • Uncontrolled Diabetes or High Blood Pressure.
  • Minors: Must be 18+, even with parental consent.
  • Using Blood Thinners: Increases bleeding risk.
  • Skin Diseases: Psoriasis or eczema in the treatment area.
  • Allergies to Pigments.
  • Active Skin Cancer: Must be 6 months post-chemo or 1 week before treatment.
  • Antibiotics: Must wait 1 day after finishing.
  • Thyroid Conditions: OK if taking thyroxine.
  • Scars: Must be at least 6 months old.
  • Moles: Treatment only around the mole. Doctor’s clearance required for lip moles.

FAQ

  • How long does PMU last? 11–18 months, lips can last 2–3 years.
  • How long is the procedure? Up to 2.5 hours depending on skin type.
  • Healing time? 6–8 days surface healing, color settles after 28 days. Top-up after 4–5 weeks.
  • Possible side effects? Swelling, redness, discomfort, rare allergic reactions (patch test done).
  • Lip injections? OK after 8–9 weeks.
  • Deposit required? Yes. Must cancel within 48 hours to keep deposit.
  • Does it hurt? Emla numbing cream is used to reduce pain.
